Armor protects you from the physical dangers of Haven—but only up to a point. It is a combination of protective gear and sacrificial shielding, designed to blunt danger or save your life once before breaking. Armor interacts with two parts of the damage system:

Pressure

Harm (physical injuries only)

Armor reduces some Pressure on a regular basis but can typically only prevent physical harm once.

Armor + Pressure

Armor can protect against most of the straightforward and physical types of Pressure. For example, Pressure induced by a fleshwound from gunfire, a collapsing walkway, or falling from a roof could all be reduced by armor.

Unless otherwise stated, armor does not typically disrupt Pressure arising from other sources - such as emotional reactions (e.g. panic, anger, grief) or the otherworldly dangers of Haven (e.g. psychic emanations, Breath-related phenomenon, Maker powered energies).

These other sources of Pressure are reduced by some playbook moves, character traits, other gear, or highly specialized armor.



Armor + Harm

When you receive a physical form of Harm, you may choose to sacrifice the armor you are wearing. Unless otherwise stated:

Harm is prevented.

The armor becomes broken and immediately loses all protective qualities. Armor cannot reduce Pressure until repaired or be used to prevent any additional Harm. See Tinkering in the downtime moves section for more about repairs.
