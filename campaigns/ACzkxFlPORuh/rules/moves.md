Moves are the rules that step in when the story turns uncertain, dangerous, or charged with consequence. They don’t dictate what you can do—your choices in the story always come first. Moves resolve what happens when those choices carry risk, pressure, or meaningful stakes.

When a move is triggered, follow its instructions and resolve the result. A strong hit means you get what you were trying to do. A mixed success introduces complications or costs. A miss always pushes the situation forward, often through escalation.

This game uses three kinds of moves:

* **Field Moves** resolve what individual characters do under pressure—acting, reacting, supporting one another, and navigating danger in the moment.

* **Downtime Moves** handle recovery, repair, reflection, and longer-term choices made between missions or major scenes.

* **Ship Moves** cover actions taken at the scale of the crew’s vessel—maneuvering, weathering storms, pushing systems, and surviving Haven’s skies.

Together, these moves support play that is fast, fiction-driven, and consequence-focused. They exist to keep the story moving, not to constrain it.
