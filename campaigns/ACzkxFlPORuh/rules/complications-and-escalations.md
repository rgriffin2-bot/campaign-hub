Complications and escalations are how the game applies pressure and when you don’t fully succeed.

A complication follows a mixed success: the crew achieves what they set out to do, but the way they do it introduces a new problem, cost, or constraint.

An escalation follows a miss or pressure overload: the crew loses control of the situation, and danger, urgency, or cost meaningfully increases.

Both arise from the fiction. Generally, they should be immediate, concrete, and focused on the current scene—adding friction, sharpening threats, or changing the situation without derailing play.



| Narrative Hooks | Narrative Hooks |

| --- | --- |

| Human Error A bad call under pressure. Panic, distraction, or misjudgment creates an immediate problem. | Mis-enter a code or hit the wrong control. Drop or lose a vital item. Say the wrong thing at the wrong time. Misjudge timing or positioning. Attempt a shortcut that backfires. |

| System Strain Human-built tech falters when pushed past its limits. | Weapon jams or overheats. Suit alarms trigger (leak, crack, contamination). Tools short out or burn up. Doors freeze, gravity flickers, thrusters misfire. Comms degrade or cut out. |

| Environmental Instability The place itself turns hostile or unpredictable. | Floors collapse, ledges give way, hulls tear, pressure shifts. Weather surges, ion squalls arc, toxic mist floods the area. Lighting, piping, or structural elements rupture. |

| Prepared Opposition Someone anticipated trouble and set defenses in advance. | Traps trigger. Ambush positions activate. Lockdowns engage. Turrets or drones deploy. Reinforcements move immediately. Surveillance flags your arrival. |

| Alien or Inscrutable Forces Haven’s deeper systems react on their own terms. | Maker artifacts awaken. Steward constructs initiate protocols. Breath anomalies warp space or perception. Ancient systems execute forgotten routines. Geometry distorts or reality “misbehaves.” |

| Depletion You run out of something critical at the worst moment. | Ammo clicks empty. O₂ drops into the red. Power cells drain. Medkits are already spent. Batteries fail mid-task. Fuel or coolant leaks force abandonment. |

| Bad Intel Your assumptions or data prove wrong. | The threat is larger or different than expected. The “safe” route is monitored or trapped. Signals split or mislead. Maps are outdated. Sensors misread hidden voids or structures. |

| Emotional Fallout Feelings interfere when clarity is needed most. | Panic, anger, grief, or fear cause hesitation or overreach. A memory or symbol distracts you. Someone in danger pulls you off-plan. Trauma triggers a reflexive mistake. |

| Betrayal or Abandonment Someone you relied on turns, hesitates, or saves themselves. | An ally withdraws support. A contact lies or omits key info. A faction flips allegiance. An NPC panics or defects. A trusted AI reclassifies you as a threat. |

| Mechanical Hooks | Mechanical Hooks |

| --- | --- |

| Add Pressure | Mark Pressure on a character or the ship to represent mounting strain, distraction, or danger. Use this when the problem taxes endurance or focus rather than causing immediate damage. 1 — Minor strain or distraction. 2 — Sustained effort or compounding difficulty. 3 — Serious overload. 4 — Critical strain. 5 — Overload. Convert this into Harm or a full escalation immediately. |

| Inflict Harm | Apply Harm when the consequence represents a serious physical, psychological, or functional injury that can’t be shrugged off. Harm should feel decisive and sticky. Mild — Painful or disorienting, but still functional. Limits options or effectiveness. Moderate — Clearly debilitating. Acting normally is difficult or dangerous. Severe — Crippling or life-threatening. Immediate danger, loss of function. |

| Ship Damage | Apply Minor/Major Damage when systems, hull integrity, or ship-scale operations are compromised. This should alter how the ship can act going forward. |

| Apply or Change Gear Tags | Add negative tags or remove helpful ones. Use this to make equipment unreliable, risky, or temporarily unusable. |

| Worsen Position | Change the crew’s fictional positioning: separated, pinned, exposed, off-route, out of cover, or on a ticking clock. This limits options without directly harming anyone. |

| Advance a Threat | Bring an existing danger closer: reinforcements move, weather intensifies, systems lock down, attention sharpens. Make something that was looming become immediate. |

| Force a Choice | Present a hard trade-off: protect people or progress, speed or safety, secrecy or control, ship or crew. Make the cost explicit and immediate. |

| Close or Open Options | Remove an easy path (an exit seals, comms die) or create a narrow one (a risky escape window, a single viable solution). Either way, shape the decision space. |

| Shift Control | Take initiative away from the crew: the system acts autonomously, an NPC seizes momentum, or the environment dictates the next beat. The crew must react. |
