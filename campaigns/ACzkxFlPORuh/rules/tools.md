Tools are gear designed to solve problems, extend capability, or interact with the world in ways the human body alone cannot.

In Haven, tools are just as important as weapons. Crews survive by repairing failing systems, navigating hostile environments, bypassing obstacles, extracting information, stabilizing crises, and making hard problems tractable under pressure. Tools do that work.

A tool might be:

diagnostic or analytical,

industrial or medical,

exploratory, manipulative, or protective,

subtle or conspicuous,

mundane or deeply strange.

Like weapons, tools are defined by their tags. A scanner, a cutting torch, a sealed med-kit, and a Breath-responsive engine interface may all be tools—but they shape play in radically different ways because of how their tags interact with the fiction.

Tools do not guarantee success. They shape what you can attempt and how risky it is. A tool might grant permission to act, open new approaches, reduce or redirect danger, or—when its advantages or limitations clearly matter—inform a context modifier on the roll. Tools rarely remove uncertainty entirely.

Under stress, tools can fail, degrade, attract attention, or introduce new complications. Their tags apply in both directions: what helps in one situation may become a liability in another.

Importantly, many tools can become weapons in the right circumstances—and many weapons can be used as tools. The game does not draw a hard mechanical line between them. What matters is intent and effect in the fiction, and how the Keeper reflects that choice through consequences, positioning, or context.
