A project does not advance automatically with downtime or travel unless the fiction clearly supports it.

When you spend focused time pushing a project forward during downtime—through labor, experimentation, negotiation, planning, or fabrication—you trigger Move the Needle. Each successful roll marks progress on the clock. Progress represents meaningful advancement, not just time passing: breakthroughs, completed components, secured materials, solved problems, or locked-in commitments.

In addition, some projects require resources, materials, access, or expertise to begin or continue. The Keeper may require resources to be spent up front, during the project, or to overcome specific obstacles.
