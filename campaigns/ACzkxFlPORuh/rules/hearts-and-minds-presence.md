When you try to change an NPC’s behavior—through persuasion, intimidation, deception, or leverage—state what you want and your approach.

---

**10+ —** They’ll do what you asked. If what you want strains their limits, they may require a follow-up form of convincing or refuse outright, depending on the fiction.

* Persuade: They want proof, reassurance, or an uncomfortable compromise.

* Intimidate: They demand a clear show of force or proof you’re the greater threat.

* Deceive: They aren’t fully convinced; you must commit to the lie in a costly way.

* Manipulate: They agree, but only in exchange for immediate leverage, a favor, or risk.

**7–9 —** They’ll do it, but only if you provide a follow-up form of convincing, chosen by the Keeper and grounded in the approach you used.

**Miss —** Your approach is wrong —it backfires or reveals weakness. The Keeper escalates the situation; mark XP.

---

Note: You can’t force another crew member to act against their will. If they choose to go along, they get +1 forward.
