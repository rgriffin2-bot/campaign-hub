A long-term project is an extended effort that cannot be completed in a single scene and requires sustained progress over time. Long-term projects are tracked with project clocks, each clock representing a discrete portion of the work: a breakthrough, construction phase, negotiation, or critical milestone.

Most projects use a single clock. Large or complex projects may be broken into multiple phases, each with its own clock. Completing one phase unlocks the next, often changing what is possible, what resources are required, or what risks are involved.
