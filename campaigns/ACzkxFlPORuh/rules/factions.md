Faction standing reflects how a group—organization, belief system, settlement, corporation, flotilla, or informal network—relates to the crew over time. It measures willingness to act for or against you when it matters, not popularity or reputation.

Standing is tracked on a scale from +3 to –3. Each faction has its own standing with the crew.

Standing changes only when the fiction supports it: completing or sabotaging faction goals, public alignment or betrayal, major fallout from a mission, or Keeper-driven faction clocks. It does not change for minor favors or casual interaction.

---

**+3 Allies.** This faction considers you trusted partners.

> They will take meaningful risks on your behalf, even when it costs them politically, materially, or socially. Once per arc, they will intervene decisively to help you—providing cover, leverage, protection, or access that would normally be impossible.<br> <br> They expect loyalty in return. Refusing a major request or publicly undermining them will almost certainly reduce this standing.`

**+2 Friendly.** This faction views you as reliable and worth supporting.

> They will help you as long as doing so does not create serious problems for them. Basic access to their resources, services, or decision-makers is usually available without a roll.<br> <br> They expect respect and reciprocity. Repeatedly benefiting without giving back will strain the relationship.

**+1 Helpful.** This faction is willing to assist when the cost is low.

> They may provide minor favors, information, or access, but will not go out of their way or absorb losses for you.<br> <br> They expect you to behave similarly toward them.

**0 Neutral.** This faction is indifferent to you.

> They will not help or hinder you intentionally, but systems, procedures, and social inertia default against you. Access requires time, leverage, negotiation, or risk.<br> <br> Neutrality is not safety—it is the absence of consideration.

**–1 Interfering.** This faction sees you as a nuisance or opportunity.

> They look for safe ways to inconvenience you, profit from your misfortune, or undermine your efforts, as long as it costs them little. The Keeper may introduce faction-based complications without a miss when you operate in their sphere of influence.<br> <br> They expect similar treatment from you and will react if you escalate.

**–2: Adversarial.** This faction actively works against you.

> They will take steps to block your access, spread warnings or rumors, apply pressure through allies, and prepare defenses against you, as long as doing so does not cause serious backlash.<br> <br> The first time each session this faction interferes directly, the crew must mark 1 Pressure to reflect the added strain of operating under active opposition.

**–3 Open Hostilities.** This faction considers you an active threat.

> They will act against you even at a cost to themselves. Safe options vanish in their domain. Bureaucracies flag you, doctrines condemn you, enforcement bodies prioritize you, and indirect pressure escalates rapidly.<br> <br> While you are in Open Hostilities with one or more factions:<br> - Hostile complications escalate faster and hit harder<br> - Denial of access, systemic retaliation, or coordinated opposition may occur without a miss<br> - The world tightens around you in that faction’s sphere of influence<br> <br> Open hostilities do not end on their own. It ends only when the relationship is fundamentally restructured in the fiction, through one of the following:<br> - Capitulation: one side is forced to back down<br> - Settlement: a negotiated, brokered, or ritualized agreement is reached<br> - Containment: one side loses the ability or incentive to continue hostilities<br> - Erasure: a specific hostile node or organization is dismantled (rare and consequential)<br> <br> When Open Hostilities ends, establish a new standing that reflects what was lost, conceded, or remembered. Even resolution leaves scars.

| Belief-Driven (religions, Steward cults, Breath mystics) | Ritual welcome, public endorsement, protection by doctrine, omens interpreted favorably | Social distance, withheld blessings, silence | Condemnation, excommunication, declared heresy, public ritual opposition, social exclusion |

| Frontier / Informal (nomadic flotillas, pirate enclaves, settlements) | Hospitality, warnings shared, backup offered, safe harbor | Cautious distance, “not our problem” | Ambushes, sabotage, harassment, sudden hostility framed as “local business” |

| Security Forces (militias, patrol fleets, enforcement arms) | Patrols look the other way, backup arrives fast, warnings instead of penalties | By-the-book enforcement | Surveillance, detentions, pursuit, use of force justified as “procedure” |

| Political / Coalition Bodies (alliances, councils, blocs) | Advocacy in deliberations, agenda protection, behind-the-scenes leverage | Ignored in votes, no one sticks their neck out | Formal sanctions, bloc pressure, collective retaliation |

| Criminal / Illicit Networks (smugglers, fixers, black markets) | Information flows, safe contacts, clean deals | Cash-only, limited trust | Burned contacts, bounties, traps disguised as opportunities |
