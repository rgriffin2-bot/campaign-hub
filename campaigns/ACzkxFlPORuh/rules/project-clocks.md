A project clock represents how close a phase of a long-term effort is to completion. Each clock is divided into a number of boxes, typically 4, 6, or 8, depending on the scope and difficulty of the work:

4 boxes — Straightforward or well-supported work

6 boxes — Complex, risky, or unfamiliar work

8 boxes — Major undertakings, rare materials, or high-stakes goals

The Keeper sets the clock size when the project is established, based on the fiction.

When all boxes on a project clock are filled, the project phase is complete and its results enter the fiction.
