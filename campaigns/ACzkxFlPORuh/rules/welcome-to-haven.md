Haven is not a quiet, cold place.

It is a living star system where worlds share the same sky, where ships sail rivers of air between planets, and where storms can reshape history. Space here is not empty—it breathes, shifts, remembers. Humanity did not build this place, but it has learned to live inside it.

You play people who make their lives in that vast and wild miracle.

You are a crew: explorers, pilots, engineers, fixers, believers, skeptics, survivors. You take jobs, protect what matters to you, and push into uncertain and consequence laden places. You move between bustling cities and half-forgotten ruins, between safe harbors and regions of sky that have never been mapped twice the same way.

Haven is full of forces larger than any one person—ancient machines still maintaining the system, bio-mechanical stewards that shape weather and fate, and the Breath itself, a living medium that connects every world and every body within it. These forces are not villains to be defeated or puzzles to be solved once and for all. They are context. Pressure. Weather. The rules of the sky.

The stories you tell here aren’t about conquering Haven. They’re about navigating it — together.
