Harm represents lasting injury or impairment—physical, psychological, or situational—that goes beyond momentary strain. Harm persists across scenes and continues to matter until it is healed or stabilized. Harm is tracked in three severities: Mild, Moderate, and Severe. You can hold only one Harm at each severity. When you would mark Harm at a severity you already have, it escalates to the next highest severity instead. If a crew member has all three severities of Harm OR they receive Harm into a severity level that they aren’t able to upgrade, they are taken out of action. In this state, they can no longer act until brought back by care, intervention, or a move like Back from the Brink. Harm is fiction first. Each Harm is named to reflect what is actually wrong (burned lungs, shattered nerve, panic spiral), and that description informs the fiction, available actions, and any context modifiers that apply while it remains. When Harm is healed or downgraded, it often becomes an Old Wound—a lingering reminder that no longer fills a Harm slot but may still matter in specific situations.
