Dice are not rolled for every action. Most of play happens through conversation: description, clarification, and response. Dice only enter the picture when the outcome of an action is uncertain and consequential.

You Roll Dice When:

An action carries real risk. Failure, complication, or escalation would meaningfully change the situation.

The outcome isn’t obvious from the fiction alone. Success isn’t guaranteed, and failure wouldn’t be trivial or boring.

A move is clearly triggered. The action matches the trigger of a move, and the move’s results would help resolve what happens next.

When these conditions are met, the Keeper will identify the move (or procedure) that applies and call for a roll.

You Do Not Roll Dice When:

The action is routine, safe, or uncontested. If nothing is pushing back, the character simply does it.

The outcome is already established by the fiction. If success or failure is a foregone conclusion, the story moves forward without rolling.

Rolling wouldn’t add tension or meaning. If a roll wouldn’t change the situation in an interesting way, it’s skipped.

Rolling dice is never about permission to act. It’s about discovering what it costs and what it changes when you act under pressure.
