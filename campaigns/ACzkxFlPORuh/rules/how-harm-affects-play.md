Harm is primarily fictional, not a predefined mechanical penalty. Its impact depends on when and how it matters. When you make a move, the Keeper considers:

Does this Harm make the action harder, riskier, or less effective?

Does it directly interfere with what you’re trying to do?

Overall, does it impact the fiction of a scene?

If so, the Harm may contribute to the context mod for the roll, restrict what you can attempt, or shape the consequences of failure. Not every Harm affects every roll. A cracked rib might matter when sprinting or fighting, but not when negotiating. A compromised identity may haunt social scenes but do nothing in a vacuum breach.

You can only have one Mild, one Moderate, and one Severe Harm active at the same time. If you would take Harm of a severity you already have, upgrade an existing Harm (Mild → Moderate, Moderate → Severe), and then replace it.

If you accumulate too much harm - all harm slots are filled or you take severe harm when you already have a severe harm - you are out of action. What that looks like depends on the situation: unconscious, captured, fleeing, stabilized but sidelined, or death.
