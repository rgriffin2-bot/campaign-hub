When you trade pressure, both sides inflict pressure on each other as part of the same exchange. You deal pressure to the target, and the target deals pressure back to you, reflecting the cost, risk, or resistance of direct confrontation. The exact amount of pressure on each side depends on the move, the fiction, and any modifiers, but unless stated otherwise, trading pressure assumes that acting against an active threat exposes you to consequences at the same time you impose them.
