When someone is taken out of action, you rush to keep someone from slipping out of the fight — dragging them to cover, applying emergency aid, talking them through shock, or otherwise buying them a few more moments — say how you’re doing it and roll 2d6 + a stat supported by the fiction.

On a 7+, they are stabilized, but none of the Harm is cleared. While stabilized, further harm or severe exertion can immediately take them out of action again.

---

**10+ —** You pull them back from the brink. They are back in action in the scene, though still badly compromised. In addition, choose one:
* You get them back on their feet fast; give them +1 forward
* You reposition them into a better spot; they end up in cover, with the high ground, or otherwise having the attention pulled away from them.
* You prevent secondary harm; their injuries won’t worsen from exertion alone in this scene.
* You buy real time; they can act normally for one critical exchange before consequences catch up.

**7–9 —** You pull them back from the brink, but it costs you. They’re back in action, and the Keeper introduces a complication tied to the effort.

**Miss —** You fail to stabilize them. They remain out of action, and the Keeper escalates the situation; mark XP.
