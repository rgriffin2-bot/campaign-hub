When the crew commits to travel between locations:

Name the destination and urgency

Choose a route type

Play through any travel scenes that emerge

Frame arrival based on what the journey left behind

Only roll ship moves when the fiction demands it—storms, pursuit, system strain, sudden danger. Most journeys resolve without dice until something presses in.



| Route Type | What It Prioritizes | What It Risks | Use This Route When… |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Open Lanes | Predictability and stability | Attention from factions, authorities, or rivals | You want the safest transit and are willing to be seen, logged, or interfered with. |

| Hard Sky | Speed and direct passage | Ship integrity through Pressure and damage | You need to move fast and are willing to hurt the ship to do it. |

| Blind Routes | Anonymity through obscurity | Information and control | You want to disappear, even if it means flying without good intel. |

| Long Way | Survival and stabilization | Time and changing circumstances | The ship or crew can’t take another hit and speed doesn’t matter. |
