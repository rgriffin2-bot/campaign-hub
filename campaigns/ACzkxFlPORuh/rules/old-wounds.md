When Harm is treated and no longer active, it doesn’t necessarily vanish. Serious consequences leave traces. An old wound is a resolved Harm that still lingers in the fiction.

It no longer counts against your active Harm limits, but it continues to shape how you move through the world. They inform descriptions, complications, and Keeper moves, may limit or expand what you can plausibly attempt, and can resurface under pressure, stress, or bad luck. The Keeper may:

Treat it as a fictional constraint (“You can’t sprint on that leg.”) or enabler (“That scar helps you pass as a veteran.”)

Let it justify fiction related to a roll (“You failed that roll because your hands are still shaky.”)

On more limited occasions, it to contributes to a context modifier

Examples:

A crushed leg is set and bound. For some time you’re likely to be in a cast and - even when healed - might bother you on occasion.

Neural damage is stabilized, but leaves tremors or headaches.

A burned identity is repaired, but still draws suspicion in certain circles.
