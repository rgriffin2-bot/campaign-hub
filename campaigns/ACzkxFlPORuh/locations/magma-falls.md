Magma Falls is the largest mining operation on Caldera, a sprawling industrial complex built around a series of active lava tubes. The facility extracts rare metals and minerals deposited by volcanic activity, processing them for export to the rest of the system.

## Operations

The complex operates continuously, with three shifts of workers keeping the refineries and extraction equipment running around the clock. Automated drones venture into the hottest areas, while human workers manage the processing facilities in climate-controlled sections.

### Extraction Zone
The active mining area where materials are harvested from cooling lava flows and tube walls. Extremely hazardous - only experienced miners and specialized equipment operate here.

### Processing Plant
Where raw materials are refined and prepared for transport. The facility can process several hundred tons of ore per day.

### Worker Habitation
Housing for the 400+ workers stationed at the complex. Spartan but functional, with recreational facilities to maintain morale during long rotations.

## Hazards

Working at Magma Falls is dangerous. The facility experiences regular seismic activity, toxic gas emissions, and the ever-present threat of lava flow changes. The company pays well, but turnover is high.
