# A System Without a Single Ruler
Haven never became one empire. The colony fleet that found it was a convoy of competitors, and the miracle didn’t reconcile them—it multiplied the stakes.

Today, Haven is pluralistic to the point of chaos: states, megacorps, faith coalitions, communes, nomadic flotillas, pirates, and stubborn enclaves share the same air.

## The Four Broad “Zones” of Human Power
Rather than list nations, it’s more useful to think in layers:

1. **Old worlds:** dense populations, mature infrastructure, high governance/corporate gravity.
2. **Secondary hubs:** developed worlds with strong local identities and rivalries.
3. **Frontiers:** half-mapped moons, marginal stations, mobile habitats riding lanes.
4. **Wild regions:** uncharted sky where weather, wildlife, and relics remain unknown.

Power flows outward and snaps back inward through trade, extraction, and security.
