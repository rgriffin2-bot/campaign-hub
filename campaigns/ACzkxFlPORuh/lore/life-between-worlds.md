# Everyday Life in Open Air
Baseline human technology in Haven feels near-future—advanced, practical, and warped by the fact that “space” is breathable most of the time.

The cultural default is **openness** with **fast closure**:

- public plazas on station hulls
- exposed promenade decks
- orchard terraces under alien constellations
- and, everywhere, systems designed to seal in seconds

## Storm Readiness Culture
Because the expanse can turn violent quickly, most habitats and ships share a few instincts:

- drills and alarms treated like weather forecasts
- shutters, pressure membranes, and emergency seals
- local superstitions about “reading the sky”
- social hierarchies that elevate pilots, lane-guides, and maintenance crews

## Grafted Miracles
Maker relics are often used as infrastructure anchors:
- indestructible pillars, conduits, and surfaces
- sacred “engine rooms” nobody is allowed to modify
- ship components treated like holy antiques because they work and cannot be replaced

This makes engineering partly technical and partly ritual (see [[lore:maker-relics]]).

## Travel Time
Interworld travel is measured in **days, weeks, months**. Crossing the system is a commitment. Long voyages create their own cultures: flotilla politics, shipboard law, seasonal migration.
