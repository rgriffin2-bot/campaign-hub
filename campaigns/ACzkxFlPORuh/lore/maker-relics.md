# The Middle Ground of Miracle
Maker artifacts aren’t everywhere, and they aren’t merely myth. They’re common enough to shape the world and strange enough to break minds.

They appear as:

- **Immense structures:** kilometers of seamless material that resists cutting, drilling, or melting.
- **Fragments and devices:** shards and modules with narrow, persistent functions—indestructible supports, self-repairing surfaces, stable energy channels.

## How Humans Use Them
Humans generally do not *understand* relics. They:

- **infer and imitate:** building human tech inspired by effects they can’t explain
- **adapt and integrate:** grafting Maker components into ships and habitats like black-box miracles

This produces a culture of field manuals, superstition, and practical engineering glued to unknown principles.

## Why It’s Dangerous
Reverse engineering is often partial and sometimes lethal. Relics can:

- emit radiation or toxic byproducts
- warp local space or weather
- trigger cognitive/psychic feedback
- fail catastrophically when pushed beyond their intended role

A fragment may “work” for decades and then ruin an entire district the day someone upgrades the power bus.

## Who Fights Over Them
Relics attract predictable orbits:

- corporations seeking patents and monopoly
- religious orders protecting holy sites
- preservationists warning that meddling risks destabilizing the whole system
- frontier crews looking for one good haul
