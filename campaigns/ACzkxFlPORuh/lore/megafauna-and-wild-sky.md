# Life That Doesn’t Live on Planets
Haven’s breathable expanse supports ecosystems in open sky—some as complex as oceans, some as alien as weather.

Common encounters in frontier regions include:

- **Free-floating megafauna:** “sky-whales,” filter-feeding veils, drifting leviathans
- **Predatory swarms:** coordinated clouds that strip hull-paint, then flesh
- **Migratory habitat flotillas:** human-made caravans that behave like herds
- **Asteroid terraces:** colonies carved into rock, open to sky like cliffside towns

## Intelligence Without Parity
Some non-human species show signs of intelligence—tool use, complex signaling, social structures. None match human technology, and none share a language humans can prove is mutual.

That uncertainty makes first contact stories common—and often tragic.

## How the Wild Sky Changes Conflict
Wild regions are valuable because they are:
- hard to police
- rich in relic sites
- shaped by unpredictable weather and Stewards

They attract pirates, outcasts, researchers, and faith-seekers—often all on the same ship.
