# The Visible Hands of Maintenance
Stewards are vast bio-mechanical entities—part organism, part machine, part category error. Their scale runs from “large building” to “floating continent,” with stranger outliers: invisible meshworks in forests, reef-nerves inside asteroids, sensor-tissue braided into the air itself.

They don’t answer hails. They don’t speak. They rarely acknowledge humans.

And yet they **act**.

## What They Do (From a Human Point of View)
Stewards appear to perform maintenance across Haven’s engineered ecology:

- nudging jet streams and pressure regimes
- reclaiming or rebalancing biomes
- altering local conditions around relic sites
- enforcing “compliance” with rules nobody fully knows

The key lesson from early colony ruins is blunt:

> **Stewards are not benevolent. They are functional.**

## Why People Fear Them
Because “maintenance” can look like catastrophe:

- A corrected jet stream can scour a city from the sky.
- A rebalanced biome can erase farmland.
- A foundation shift can unseat an entire habitat.

Some Stewards seem to spare settlements; others remain terrifyingly indifferent.

## How They Shape Politics
Stewards function like moving geography. Territories form around:

- predicted routes
- avoided volumes
- pilgrimage windows
- exploit opportunities when a Steward “opens” a region

That makes Steward knowledge a strategic asset (and a religious one).
