# What Haven Is
Haven is the name humans gave to a star system that refuses the usual rules of space: between worlds lies a sprawling, mostly continuous envelope of breathable atmosphere. The result is a lived-in “sky-ocean” where ships can sail, stations can sprawl in open air, and the distance between planets feels less like the void and more like weather.

Haven is not just habitable. It behaves like a maintained environment—an engineered astro-ecology that keeps working long after its builders vanished.

## How People Explain It
Haven’s origin is argued like religion and studied like physics:

- **Designed paradise:** a garden system built to be walked in.
- **Proof of mastery:** intelligence can rewrite a solar system.
- **Accidental miracle:** we arrived at something we don’t understand and now survive inside it.

Those explanations aren’t mutually exclusive in practice—most cultures in Haven live with contradictions because daily life demands it.

## What Changed Humanity
The colony fleet that found Haven didn’t arrive as one unified civilization. It came as a convoy of competing futures. Discovering a living, breathable “between” didn’t end those tensions—it gave them new terrain to fight over.

> If your campaign needs one defining fact: **Haven made “space” into a contested commons.**
