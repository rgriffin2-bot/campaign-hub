# Belief in a Built Paradise
With absent engineer-gods, continent-sized caretakers, and a symbiotic miracle in every breath, religion in Haven doesn’t stay simple. Belief overlaps with politics, science, and survival.

## Major Currents of Thought
Rather than single churches, Haven tends to produce *clusters*:

### Maker-Centric Faiths
- The Makers as distant gods
- The Makers as perfected beings to emulate
- The Makers as moral architects whose design implies purpose

### Steward Cults
Local religions devoted to particular Stewards as patrons, guardians, or judges. Often defined by:
- pilgrimage timed to predicted routes
- rituals of alignment and appeasement
- taboos against certain technologies or lane practices

### Breath Mysticism
Traditions treating the Breath as literal oneness:
- meditation as attunement practice
- ethical codes built around interconnection
- saints/prophets/heretics framed as Breath-active individuals

### Secular Rationalism
Movements determined to frame miracles as physics and biology—sometimes cooperative with faiths, sometimes aggressively anti-mystic.

### Indifference
As is often the case, there are also people who are just trying to live their life. That they are surrounded by mysteries and seeming miracles is something they've grown accustomed to - as natural as the air the breathe. They think of the Stewards much as you'd think of a storm and having big thoughts about Makers is in a minor detail in their life.
