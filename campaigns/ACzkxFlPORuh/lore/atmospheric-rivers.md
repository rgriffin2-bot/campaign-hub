# Haven’s Highways
Inside the habitable expanse, weather organizes into colossal jet streams—stable, roaring “rivers” of atmosphere that connect worlds like shipping lanes. These currents become the backbone of trade, migration, and war.

Most maps in Haven are not just geography. They are **meteorology**: route charts, seasonal lane shifts, and the reputations of particular winds.

## The Lane Economy
Sky-lanes do three jobs at once:

1. **Transportation:** sail-ships, sky-barges, caravans of linked habitats.
2. **Communication:** couriers and packet-runners riding predictable currents.
3. **Power:** whoever can predict, patrol, or gate a lane can tax it.

Lane control creates soft empires without needing to “own” planets.

## When the Lanes Fail
Outside stable currents, you get:

- **Storms the size of moons**
- **Pressure cliffs** (invisible walls that flip ships or crush hulls)
- **Luminous ion squalls** that interfere with tech and nerves alike

The line between “route” and “disaster” can be a single wrong forecast.

## Adventure Hooks
- A lane shifts, stranding a flotilla-city.
- A corporation claims it can *stabilize* a route—at a price.
- A Steward’s passage rewrites the system’s weather (see [[lore:stewards]]).
