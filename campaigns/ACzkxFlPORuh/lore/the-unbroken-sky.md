# The Shared Atmosphere
In Haven, “between planets” isn’t empty. It’s an irregular ocean of air that wraps worlds, moons, stations, and drifting habitats in a mostly continuous breathable medium.

That single detail changes everything:

- **Architecture goes outward.** Habitats grow plazas, hull-walks, orchard decks, sky-gardens.
- **Ships behave like boats.** Most days are open-railing travel; “sealing up” is a storm protocol, not a default.
- **Borders get weird.** If the sky is shared, who owns the lanes, the air, the storms, the safe harbors?

## Thin Places and Bad Pockets
The sky isn’t uniform. The system includes:

- **Thin zones:** places where the atmosphere thins until it fails into vacuum.
- **Toxic pockets:** regions where air chemistry curdles, stings, or kills.
- **Strange volumes:** localized anomalies that make bodies ache and instruments lie.

Crossing the ragged edge into hard vacuum is possible, but culturally uncommon: most people live and move within the breathable commons.

## Practical Implications for Play
Use the Unbroken Sky to make space feel *navigable* but never *safe*:

- You can meet a stranger on an open deck between worlds.
- You can lose a city to a pressure wall.
- You can build a settlement that’s “mobile” by riding the sky itself.
